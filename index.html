Hand

Exoskeleton part 1

A mechanical exoskeleton system is under implementation for housing the body motion mapping magnetic rotary encoder system. The main purpose is to provide robust attachment and positioning of the joint angle sensors so that they are located and rotated on the desired axises of the human body. The initial 3D models were crafted in a 3D modelling CAD software based on these sketches.

Further iterations of the design were done in the CAD software.

In the current design the magnetic angular sensors will be placed on the knuckle joints where the finger structure is joined to the back of the hand. The other joints together with long enough connections allow necessary movement and larger change in the knuckle joint angle when the fingers are bent compared to when the hand is opened. Large enough change in the measured joint angle is essential for the usable resolution of the angle sensor.

Arm and Shoulder

Exoskeleton part 2

First some background research was conducted on other exoskeletons and robots to find out how joints on the arms and shoulders have been implemented in other projects. Based on the research the most suitable assembly of joints was sketched out. The latest sketch is shown in Figure 8. This sketch shows four main joints: two around the shoulder, a rotational one at the bicep, and one at the elbow.

Quick mock-ups (one shown in the picture below.) were constructed based on the design in the sketches, out of cheap readily available materials such as styrofoam, cardboard and cable ties. As to be expected, the design needed some modification: To properly rotate the arm without it breaking it needed an additional rotatory joint under the shoulder blade. Two more rotational joints along the arm were also found necessary.

Simulation

Of Robot Movement

Having a simulation environment for the InMoov allows the testing of implemented features without having the access to the physical robot itself. The simulation is implemented within ROS by using a unified robot description format (URDF) -model in conjunction with publishing desired states for joints matching the physical InMoov. The model can then be visualized within RViz in real-time. The URDF-files used are cloned from a third party open-source project InMoov_Ros and the official InMoov 3D-model files are used for visualization. Model visualization is shown below.
